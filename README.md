Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
The Gaming Room was a client that wanted to expand its game, Draw It or Lose It, to multiple platforms. The game was originally designed for Android, but the company wanted a solution that would allow more users to play on different operating systems. The software design focused on creating a system that was reliable, scalable, and able to support future growth.

What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I think one of my strengths was looking at several possible solutions and comparing their advantages and disadvantages. This helped me make recommendations based on the client's needs instead of just choosing the option I was most familiar with.

What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Working through the design document helped me slow down and think about the project before jumping into development. It made me consider things like operating systems, memory management, security, and scalability early in the process. Having that plan in place made the coding portion easier to understand.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I could go back and revise part of the document, I would spend more time on the recommendations section. As I worked through the course, I gained a much better understanding of software architecture and operating systems, so I think I could provide stronger explanations and more detailed recommendations now.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I started by focusing on what The Gaming Room wanted to accomplish and used those requirements to guide the design decisions. Their goal was to reach more users and support future growth, so those needs influenced every recommendation. Considering the user's needs is important because the software should solve a real problem and provide value to the people who will actually use it.

How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
I approached the design process by breaking the project into smaller pieces and evaluating different options before making a recommendation. In the future, I would continue using design documents, diagrams, and requirement analysis before starting development. This course showed me that spending time planning can prevent a lot of issues later and lead to a better overall design.
